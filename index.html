A Flavor for Every Investor

Companies issue different types of preferred stock with special features. Understanding these is key to picking the right one for your goals.

Cumulative

If the company skips a dividend, it adds up. They must pay you all missed dividends before common stockholders get a dime.

Callable

The company can buy back the stock from you at a set price. This is a risk if interest rates fall, as it caps your potential gains.

Convertible

Gives you the option to convert your preferred shares into common stock, letting you share in the company's growth potential.

Participating

Allows you to receive extra dividends on top of your fixed rate if the company hits certain profit targets.
